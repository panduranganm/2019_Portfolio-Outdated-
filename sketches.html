Personal Project

Sketches

Sketching is a continuous path of growth and self-discovery. Over the past year, I have been making a conscious effort to set aside time every week to just sit down and draw. It's been a great way to improve my drawing abilities and destress too. I think it has also been interesting to see my goals and methods shift as I have gone through this process. In the beginning, I drew images from my preconceived notions. Looking back at that work, I see this led to organic objects having incorrect proportions or oversimplified aspects. Then, I started to draw almost solely using references. Looking back at this work, I see I've become better at drawing lines without letting my preconceived notions guide the pencil - this helped me improve at drawing human anatomy which has complex blends of harsh angles and smooth curves. While I'm still partially in this phase, I see myself drawing with more of my own ideas again, which has been interesting to notice.

To keep my momentum going, I pushed myself to make an art instagram account (shameless plug - follow @pandurart ) so that I could hold myself accountable to keep drawing even during the academic year. This step has encouraged me to be comfortable sharing my work with others. I think it was a really important roadblock for me to overcome because by opening myself up, I can hear others thoughts and take constructive criticism and grow as an artist.
