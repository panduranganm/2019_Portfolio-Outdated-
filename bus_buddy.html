Project for Design for IoT

Bus Buddy

Bus Buddy is meant to be used as wall decor in a coffee shop or public setting. It tracks the times of buses approaching a certain stop. When the bus is close, the display's miniature bus starts moving and shows how far away the real-time bus is. I worked in a group of three to build this project.

The goal of this project was to design and prototype an ambient device. An ambient device is something that meshes into the background and provides helpful information when the user chooses to acknowledge its presence. In this way, the device can be useful without feeling overbearing to the user.

You can find the full documentation of our project here.

01

Brainstorming

We began by thinking of a handful of environments. Then, we compiled a list of tasks that people do/information they need. From there, we focused on exploring how to bring IoT devices into scenarios to passively help users.

We played with the a few ideas such as a bedroom mirror that indicates the weather or a system to remember your keys. However, we wanted something that meshed with its background even more and decided to move forward with a wall display that showed when a bus was nearing. This could be installed in any setting such as a library or coffeeshop.

02

Prototyping

There were two main components to this project. My group had to build a pulley system to pull a minature bus back and forth and we also had to write code to make the bus move at correct times.

03

My Contribution

I worked on designing the pulley system and integrating a bus time API so that our device could detect when a bus was approaching.
